Activities🏆

While it is important to focus on academics in college to work for the dream career that you are working towards, it is also just as important to enjoy the hobbies and activities that you do throughout your daily life. It is important to have an enjoyable and happy experience in college as this is a time where you create many connections with people, discover more things about your own interests, and experience new things that will be rememorable for the rest of your life. Not only is it good to do activities for yourself but it also builds your character and personal profile as a well-rounded person which looks great to employers and graduate schools. There are many activities that one can participate in whether it be sports, clubs, internships, volunteering experiences, shadowing and so much more. These type of activities are a very important factor to look at when deciding which college you go to as many colleges tailor to specific types of student's interests.

Frequently Asked Questions 🙋

Finding the right activity is all based on your interests and your career goal. Picking to join a club because of interest shows that you are a person that has different traits and are willing to take a step outside of your circle to expand your connections. Going into leadership roles is greatly emphasized as this will stand out in your resume and future graduate applications. It is also important to consider the career your going into. If it's health related, it would be good to do volunteering and shadowing in the field to show expertise. If your going into business, law, or any liberal arts career maybe it is better to get involved in internships. It's all about what looks best on your application and something that you are genuinely interested in.

You can find all the activities offered by every college on their homepage. Most colleges have a dedicated page to just listing all the clubs they offer. Competitive sports leagues are done through tryouts and will most likely be done by recruiters. Finding internships may be done through announcements from certain offices of the college or can be found by a simple search. Volunteering and shadowing opportunities can be found by joining career-related clubs which give good recommendations.

It is always best to start early! When you are accepted and have decided to go to a college, plan which clubs you would be interested in so that you can eventually get leadership roles in those clubs as you gain recognition and connections over time. However, sometimes it is better to drop extracurriculars based on your schedule or your interests as many things can change over time. It is not always about being involved with many experiences rather it is committing time and dedication to few activities showing growth and empowerment over time.
