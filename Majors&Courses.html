Majors 🧑‍🏫

The biggest factor in deciding what you will do in college is your major as this helps to pick the courses that are required of you. Thinkng about your major and deciding what you are going to do can be challenging as you may not know what you want to do with your career yet. You are stuck in this place thinking you can't go to college without a plan. A few ways to start thinking about what you want to major in is finding out what you are interested in through the careers you think you could go into for the rest of your life so it's always best to get hands-on experience or real-life shadowing in different professions. Connecting your interests to school can help by connecting the hobbies that you are interested in. You can even go undecided for the first year of college as you take general requirements but at one point you will eventually have to pick a major that works best for you. Thankfully, we have attached some quizzes and some popular majors throughout America so you can get some guidance about which major you could pick.

Standardized Exams 📝

For careers that require higher education and sometimes for some jobs, standardized exams are required to be taken. No matter what degree you go into, you will have to face exams in college as they are the most common way to test your knowledge. Standardized exams are just upscaled models of those exams but usually contain a greater extent of information while needing to study for it outside of the time spent for college and other activities. It is important to figure out which exams you need to take for the professional career you want to go into and plan from ahead when and where you should take the exam. To learn more about which tests you may have to take, please go to the website below and learn about how to study and when to start studying for those exams so you can be prepared.

Courses

After picking a major and deciding which college you will go to, it is now time to pick the courses that you need to take. However, this can be very stressful as you need to figure out which classes you need to take and when to take them. It gets even harder if it is a bigger college as there are much more classes to choose from with more people to compete with. This is why you should schedule which courses you need to take from beforehand with an excel sheet. You can figure out what classes you need to take based on the classes you need for your degree or based on the prerequisites that are needed for your target graduate schools.
