Standardized Exams üìù

For careers that require higher education and sometimes for some jobs, standardized exams are required to be taken. No matter what degree you go into, you will have to face exams in college as they are the most common way to test your knowledge. Standardized exams are just upscaled models of those exams but usually contain a greater extent of information while needing to study for it outside of the time spent for college and other activities. It is important to figure out which exams you need to take for the professional career you want to go into and plan from ahead when and where you should take the exam. To learn more about which tests you may have to take, please go to the website below and learn about how to study and when to start studying for those exams so you can be prepared.

Courses üìã

After picking a major and deciding which college you will go to, it is now time to pick the courses that you need to take. However, this can be very stressful as you need to figure out which classes you need to take and when to take them. It gets even harder if it is a bigger college as there are much more classes to choose from with more people to compete with. This is why you should schedule which courses you need to take from beforehand with an excel sheet. You can figure out what classes you need to take based on the classes you need for your degree or based on the prerequisites that are needed for your target graduate schools. Some of your high school classes such as AP, IB, or dual credit classes can be transferred to your college to cover classes and give you credits. It's very important to check through the prerequisites for classes as you may need to take courses previously to get into higher level courses, take corequisites with some classes, and check the description for the requirements and level of proficiency needed for each class. It is recommended to make connections with upperclassmen or people who have previously taken the class to figure out the difficulty and the best way to succeed in the class. To make future planning easier for you, we have provided an excel sheet which allows you to document everything so you can make the right level of difficulty for each semester and build your own schedule.
